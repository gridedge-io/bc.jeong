About

Science · Investment · Ideas

I started Power × Compute to think in public about a question I can't stop working on: as AI workloads become the dominant driver of electricity demand, how does the grid adapt — and who builds what comes next?

I am a Power Systems Researcher and Energy Venture Investor working at the convergence of electricity infrastructure and artificial intelligence. My background spans engineering, policy, and investment — an unusual combination that consistently reveals new angles on how infrastructure evolves.

I hold an M.S. in Power Systems Engineering from POSTECH, with IEEE-published research on grid stability and HVDC control. I am currently a PhD candidate focused on the power dynamics of AI data centers and real-time grid control — specifically, how data centers can shift from passive loads to active grid resources.

In parallel, I work as an Investment Director at Samsung Venture Investment (CVC), evaluating startups at the AI-energy intersection: grid software, power electronics, flexible load management, and next-generation storage.

Before that, I spent five years as a Senior Researcher at KETEP (Korea Institute of Energy Technology Evaluation and Planning), a government-funded agency under the Ministry of Trade, Industry and Energy (MOTIE — 산업통상자원부). Across two divisions, I secured and managed national energy R&D budgets in coordination with MOTIE, the Ministry of Science and ICT, and the Ministry of Economy and Finance — integrating carbon neutrality and green new deal directives into R&D planning cycles. In the Electric Power Industry Division, I evaluated smart grid projects including VPP integration platforms for variable renewable energy and ISO-DSO cooperative operation systems. I also served on MOTIE's 2030 Energy Advisory Group, contributing policy recommendations on energy security, rare earth supply chains, LNG strategy, and nuclear power.

The goal of this blog: find ideas that present real value — grounded in how electricity markets actually work, how power systems actually behave, and where capital is actually needed.

Current Research Focus

  • Inference-Aware Forecasting (IAF) — a new paradigm for grid demand modeling
  • GPU dynamic power modeling (LPV, SINDy, DMDc)
  • Grid-aware computing: AI workloads as ancillary service assets
